The Grind

When I first started working on this monitor stand, I had no experience in manufacturing or product design—only a strong vision of what I wanted to create. Balancing work and this new venture was exhausting, but I spent every spare hour reading, researching, and prototyping. I wanted a monitor stand that was lightweight yet durable, one that could be set up in seconds without tools.

There were days when my designs failed completely, when prototypes crumbled under pressure or just didn’t fit my vision. But each challenge only made me more determined. With every test and redesign, I got closer to something that felt right. When I finally held the first successful prototype, I knew all the late nights and setbacks had been worth it. This wasn’t just a product to me—it was a solution I knew others needed, too.

The Spark

I’ll never forget the day the idea for designing a portable monitor stand first came to me. I was working on a client project in a crowded café, my laptop and extra monitor precariously balanced on a small table. My setup was cumbersome, and by the time I packed everything up, I was sore and frustrated. That’s when it hit me: remote work shouldn’t mean sacrificing comfort and productivity.

I envisioned a solution that would allow people like me—freelancers, students, remote workers—to create a full, professional workspace anywhere. LiteLift was born out of this dream to provide an efficient, portable monitor stand, one that would fit seamlessly into any lifestyle. Starting this journey wasn’t easy, but I was driven by the hope of making remote work just as comfortable and productive as any traditional office.
